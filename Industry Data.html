2. From your group’s ideal jobs, you can identify a set of skills required for these jobs. These can be divided into general skills.

General Skills
• Communication - Always working in a team involves great communication skills from one team member to another to ensure information is passed through efficiently and effectively.
• Leadership - In a group of people working together, someone has to take charge and lead the pack, this is why leadership would be an important skill.
• Teamwork - In the IT world most people work together on group projects to make sure the work gets done faster, this means that teamwork is an essential skill for anyone looking to work IT.
• Acceptance - The IT world has a broad range of different types of people with different races and genders, accepting these people especially in this day in age is a very important skill that must be acknowledged.
• Positive Attitude - Although failure is present in every workplace, it is most common in the IT field as there are many errors that can be faced on the way. Being able to maintain a positive attitude throughout your work is a key characteristic that most employers would look for in an individual.

IT-Specific Skills
• Experience in different web development tools and languages - Java, PHP, Ruby on Rails, Ember, Node JS, JSON
• Experience with different operating systems - Mac OS, Windows, Linux platforms.
• Experience with programming and scripting languages - Swift, Objective-C, C, C++, Python.
• Experience with software revision control tools - Github
• Experience with creating user design interfaces

3. Having looked at the Burning Glass data, has your opinion of your ideal job changed? Why or why not?

After analysing and examining the Burning Glass data, everyone’s overall opinion on their ideal job has not changed at all. They are still willing to pursue their ideal jobs. This is due to the fact that most of our ideal jobs are higher up in the Top IT Job Titles List of March 2018. With Web Developer being quite high up the on this list with around 380 job postings, the opinion on this job is still the same as it is quite high in demand and desirable. This would be a little more effortless for the individual to get their ideal job if they possessed the desired skill set. Software developer is also high up in the list with approximately 340 postings and the skills that are required for this job is also desirable for many other jobs. So if this job does not work out, the individual can easily find another job that would need similar skills. The remaining ideal jobs such as UI Designer, IT Operations and Information Architect are somewhere in the middle of the least or towards the end, however the desired skills are all useful within other jobs if ever the individual makes an attempt for a higher experienced job.
